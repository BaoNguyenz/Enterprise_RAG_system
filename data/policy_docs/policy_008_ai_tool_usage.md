# AI Tool Usage Policy
**Document Type:** Policy
**Policy ID:** POL-008
**Owner:** IT Department / Legal / CISO
**Effective Date:** 2024-06-01
**Regulation:** GDPR Article 22, EU AI Act

## Purpose
Govern the approved use of Artificial Intelligence tools by TechDocs employees to maximize productivity while protecting company data and ensuring regulatory compliance.

## Stakeholders
| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| CISO | Risk assessment of AI tools |
| Legal Department | Contract review, EU AI Act compliance |
| DPO | Data privacy review for AI tool data flows |
| IT Department | Approved tool list maintenance, access provisioning |
| HR Department | Training and awareness programs |
| Managers | Monitor team AI usage, approve exceptions |
| All Employees | Use only approved tools, report incidents |

## Approved AI Tools
| Tool | Use Case | Data Classification Allowed | Approval Level |
|------|---------|---------------------------|---------------|
| TechDocs AI Assistant (TDAI-2024) | Internal doc Q&A | Confidential | All employees |
| GitHub Copilot | Code generation | Internal code | Engineers only |
| Claude (Enterprise) | Drafting, summarization | Internal, Confidential | All employees |
| Grammarly Business | Writing assistance | Public, Internal | All employees |

## Prohibited Actions
- Inputting customer PII into any AI tool not listed above
- Using personal AI accounts (e.g., free ChatGPT) for work tasks
- Accepting AI-generated code without security review
- Using AI to make autonomous decisions affecting employees (hiring, performance)
- Bypassing this policy by routing data through third-party intermediaries

## Data Classification for AI Input
| Classification | Can Enter Into Approved AI | Notes |
|---------------|--------------------------|-------|
| Public | Yes | No restrictions |
| Internal | Yes | Approved tools only |
| Confidential | Limited | TDAI and Claude Enterprise only |
| Restricted (PII, financial) | No | Prohibited in all AI tools |

## EU AI Act Compliance
TechDocs classifies internal AI deployments per EU AI Act risk tiers:
- TDAI-2024: Limited risk — transparency disclosure required
- Automated hiring tools: High risk — human oversight mandatory
- Customer profiling: High risk — DPO review required

## Incident Reporting
Report AI-related incidents (data leakage, unexpected outputs) to: ai-incidents@techdocs.com
